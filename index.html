IT'S FINE.

An official communiqué from Natalie

If you are reading this, you have recently received something from Natalie — a message, an image, a gif, perhaps a frog with a firearm — that may have registered somewhere on the spectrum between "extremely direct" and "I should check on her."

You do not need to check on her.

What you witnessed was simply Natalie expressing herself at her natural intensity — a force that, whether delivered as a strongly worded sentence, a pointed reaction, or a cartoon character doing something that felt oddly specific, was intended in the spirit of humor, camaraderie, and general workplace enthusiasm.

The image was not a threat assessment. The message was not a warning shot. It was a normal form of communication from someone whose expressive range simply runs a little hotter than average, which is, by all accounts, a feature, not a bug.

She means it in a humorous way. She appreciates your concern. No alarms need to be raised. The building is fine. You are fine. We are all fine.

You may now return to your regularly scheduled afternoon. Natalie will continue to be exactly like this.

— Natalie, who is, to be very clear, doing great ⚡
